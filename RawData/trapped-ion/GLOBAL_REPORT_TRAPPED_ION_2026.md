## Executive summary

Trapped-ion is one of the most mature gate-based quantum modalities, and is now in an early commercialization phase.

Global leadership is currently concentrated in:
1. United States
2. United Kingdom
3. Austria
4. Germany
5. Japan

Fast-rising and strategically important:
- South Korea
- Switzerland
- China
- India
- Australia

## Who the major companies are

Core trapped-ion hardware/commercial leaders:
- IonQ (US; expanded UK footprint after Oxford Ionics acquisition)
- Quantinuum (US-UK; H-Series trapped-ion systems)
- Oxford Ionics (UK, now part of IonQ)
- AQT Alpine Quantum Technologies (Austria)
- Universal Quantum (UK/Germany deployment footprint)
- QUDORA / eleQtron ecosystem (Germany)
- ZuriQ (Switzerland, early-stage trapped-ion startup)

## Government policy patterns driving trapped-ion innovation

Common policy levers across leading countries:
1. National quantum strategies with multi-year budgets.
2. Public procurement/testbeds (national labs, data-center deployments).
3. Public-private contracts to build in-country hardware capability.
4. University-to-startup translation funding.
5. Workforce and industrial ecosystem programs.

## Outlook to 2030 (high level)

Most likely path:
- Trapped-ion remains a top architecture for high-fidelity, error-correction-oriented progress.
- Hybrid HPC + trapped-ion deployments expand in national labs and applied research centers.
- Commercial value initially concentrates in high-value verticals (chemistry, optimization, security-adjacent workloads).

Key determinant by 2030:
- Countries that combine policy continuity, deployment infrastructure, and startup/commercial execution will lead.
