## Executive summary

Topological quantum computing has the potential for lower-overhead fault tolerance, but it remains a high-uncertainty, high-upside path. By 2030, the most likely outcome is **meaningful technical progress with selective early deployments**, not broad mainstream production at scale.

## Where the technology is headed

1. **From evidence to engineering**: the field is shifting from Majorana signature evidence to reproducible qubit operations and error-corrected primitives.
2. **Materials and device quality race**: progress depends heavily on topological superconductor fabrication, low-noise control, and reproducible nanowire behavior.
3. **Validation standardization**: independent benchmarks and cross-lab reproducibility will determine which claims convert to durable leadership.
4. **Hybrid strategy**: topological research will coexist with superconducting, trapped-ion, and neutral-atom paths; commercial users will adopt whichever reaches reliable logical performance first.

## Scenarios (2026-2030)

### Base case
- Topological platforms deliver stronger validation and limited logical demonstrations.
- Commercial traction remains concentrated in public-sector and strategic R&D programs.

### Upside case
- Clear reproducible topological-qubit operations plus robust error-correction milestones by 2028-2029.
- Early sovereign and industrial deployments accelerate by 2030.

### Downside case
- Reproducibility and fabrication challenges delay practical utility.
- Capital and policy shift toward faster-moving alternative qubit modalities.

## Risks to monitor

1. Experimental reproducibility across independent labs.
2. Over-interpretation of early results before robust peer-validated milestones.
3. Supply-chain constraints for specialized materials/fabrication.
4. Talent concentration in a few labs/companies.

## Leading indicators through 2030

1. Demonstrated topological qubit operations replicated by multiple groups.
2. Peer-reviewed benchmark improvements in error rates and stability.
3. Repeat national procurement and sustained private co-investment.
4. University-to-startup translation in topological-specific hardware.

## Bottom line

Topological quantum computing remains one of the most strategically important frontier bets in quantum. If current validation milestones translate into reproducible engineering performance, it could become a major 2030-era architecture; if not, value will remain concentrated in fundamental science and enabling technologies.
