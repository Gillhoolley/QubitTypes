# Denmark

## Country status

Denmark is a top global enabling ecosystem for topological qubits through Copenhagen materials/device research.

## Government policy and support

- National Strategy for Quantum Technology (2023) plus long-horizon public investment commitments.
- Infrastructure funding supports access, skills, and experimentation.

## Universities and research institutions

- Niels Bohr Institute, Center for Quantum Devices (QDev), University of Copenhagen.
- QDev explicitly includes topological qubit research.

## Companies investing and doing R&D

- Copenhagen hosts strong translational pathways, including historical Microsoft Quantum Lab collaboration.

## Standout milestones

- QDev hosted Microsoft Quantum Lab (2018-2021), with joint work on engineered topological superconductors.
