# Topological Quantum Computing - Global Report (2026)

Last updated: 2026-03-07

## Scope

This report focuses on **topological-qubit pathways** (Majorana/topological superconductivity approaches for fault-tolerant quantum computing), including policy support, university research depth, company activity, and deployment readiness.

Countries covered:
- Australia
- China
- Denmark
- Germany
- Japan
- Netherlands
- Switzerland
- United Kingdom
- United States

## Executive summary

Topological quantum computing is strategically important but still at an earlier commercialization stage than superconducting and trapped-ion platforms.

### Key global picture

- The field is **highly concentrated**, with one dominant industrial effort: **Microsoft**.
- The strongest enabling research clusters are in **United States, Netherlands, and Denmark**, with important contributions from **Germany, Japan, and China**.
- National quantum strategies are increasingly funding this area via materials, devices, and test infrastructure.

### Countries leading the change (2026)

1. United States
2. Netherlands
3. Denmark
4. Germany
5. Japan
6. Switzerland

## Global companies involved

Core topological-qubit companies/programs:
- **Microsoft** (Majorana/topological qubit program; Majorana 1 announcement in 2025)

Important adjacent/validation players:
- **Quantinuum** (created/controlled non-Abelian topological states on trapped-ion hardware for algorithmic/topological demonstrations)
- University spinout ecosystems in Delft/Copenhagen/Wurzburg regions (early-stage, mostly research-translation rather than scaled topological hardware products)

## Government policy drivers (global pattern)

Most common levers:
1. National quantum strategy with multi-year funding.
2. Mission-oriented grants for quantum materials and topological superconductivity.
3. University-industry translation support and infrastructure funding.
4. Security and strategic-tech framing to maintain sovereign capability.

## Important caveat
