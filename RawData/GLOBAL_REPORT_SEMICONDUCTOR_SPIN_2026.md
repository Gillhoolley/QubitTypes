# Semiconductor Spin-Qubit Quantum Computing - Global Report (2026)

Last updated: 2026-03-07

## Scope

This report covers quantum computing based on semiconductor spin qubits (silicon and closely related semiconductor-spin platforms), including policy support, university research, industrial activity, and commercialization readiness.

Countries covered:
- Australia
- Belgium
- China
- Finland
- France
- Germany
- Ireland
- Japan
- Netherlands
- United Kingdom
- United States

## Executive summary

Semiconductor spin qubits are moving from academic prototypes to early industrialization. The central strategic advantage is compatibility with existing semiconductor manufacturing, especially 300mm CMOS flows.

Top countries leading the change (2026):
1. United States
2. Australia
3. United Kingdom
4. Netherlands
5. France
6. Germany

Fast-rising:
- Ireland
- Finland
- Japan

## Major global companies and institutions involved

## Policy drivers globally

Common government levers:
1. National quantum strategies and mission funding.
2. Semiconductor-compatible hardware development grants.
3. National testbed programs and public procurement.
4. University spinout support and venture co-investment.
5. Sovereign technology and supply-chain resilience policies.

## 2030 direction (high-level)

Most likely by 2030:
- Semiconductor spin qubits become one of the primary scalable hardware tracks for fault-tolerant ambitions.
- Value concentrates around foundry integration, cryo-CMOS control, and full-stack deployment readiness.
- Countries with both strong quantum science and semiconductor-industrial depth capture the most value.
