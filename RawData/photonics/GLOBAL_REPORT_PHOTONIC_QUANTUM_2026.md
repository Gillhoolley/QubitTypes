# Global Quantum Photonics Report (2026)

Last updated: 2026-03-07

## Scope

This report focuses on **photonic-qubit quantum computing** (and closely related photonic quantum hardware that underpins computing pathways), with country-level policy, research, and commercialization signals.

Countries covered:
- Australia
- Canada
- China
- France
- Germany
- Israel
- Japan
- Netherlands
- Singapore
- South Korea
- United Kingdom
- United States

## Executive picture

Photonic quantum computing has moved from lab prototypes to a commercialization phase driven by:
- national quantum strategies,
- public procurement/testbeds,
- photonic manufacturing and packaging investment,
- university spinout pipelines.

### Countries leading today

Top tier by combined policy + industry + research depth:
1. United States
2. United Kingdom
3. Canada
4. China
5. Netherlands
6. Japan

Fast-rising with strong policy or deployment momentum:
- Australia
- Singapore
- France
- South Korea

## Global companies active in photonic quantum computing

Core photonic-qubit leaders:
- PsiQuantum (US, major UK and Australia footprint)
- Xanadu (Canada)
- Quandela (France)
- QuiX Quantum (Netherlands)
- ORCA Computing (UK)
- Quantum Source (Israel)

Supporting and adjacent ecosystem players with photonic relevance:
- Photonic Inc. (Canada, photonically linked architecture)
- GlobalFoundries ecosystem partners (US/global manufacturing relevance)
- NTT and OptQC (Japan optical quantum collaboration)

## What governments are doing globally

Most common policy levers:
1. National quantum strategy funding envelopes.
2. Public procurement and testbeds (government labs, national centers).
3. Public-private co-funding for scale-up and manufacturing.
4. University-to-startup translation support.
5. National security framing (resilience, cryptography, strategic tech sovereignty).

## Where this is heading (2026-2029)

Most likely near-term outcomes:
- More hybrid HPC-photonic deployments in public infrastructure.
- Increased focus on component supply chain sovereignty (packaging, PICs, cryo integration where needed).
- Faster market pull in quantum-safe security and optimization workloads ahead of full fault-tolerant systems.
