## Executive summary

By 2030, semiconductor spin qubits are likely to be one of the leading hardware paths for large-scale fault-tolerant quantum ambitions, primarily because they can leverage mature CMOS/foundry ecosystems.

## Where the technology is headed

1. **Foundry-first scaling**: more devices fabricated on 300mm-compatible processes.
2. **Cryo-CMOS integration**: control electronics moving closer to qubits becomes a major bottleneck-breaker.
3. **2D array expansion**: shift from small linear demonstrations toward larger two-dimensional arrays for error correction.
4. **Systemization**: from qubit metrics to full-stack systems (software, control, deployment footprint).

## Scenarios (2026-2030)

### Base case
- Semiconductor-spin reaches repeatable high-fidelity operations and early fault-tolerant building blocks.
- Government labs and strategic enterprise pilots drive first commercial value.

### Upside case
- Fast progress in cryo-CMOS and error correction accelerates utility-scale systems.
- Multiple countries launch production-grade public procurement programs before 2030.

### Downside case
- Scaling challenges in control complexity and packaging slow deployment.
- Competing modalities reach production utility faster in key workloads.

## Risks to watch

1. Integration complexity between qubits and classical control at scale.
2. Yield/reproducibility in scaled qubit arrays.
3. Supply-chain and geopolitical constraints in semiconductor manufacturing.
4. Conversion gap between benchmark claims and paid production use.

## Leading indicators

1. Number of paid on-prem or sustained cloud contracts.
2. Reproducibility across randomly sampled fabricated devices.
3. National procurement repeat cycles.
4. Measured logical performance improvements, not only physical-qubit metrics.

## Bottom line

Semiconductor spin qubits have one of the clearest industrialization pathways in quantum computing. Countries that combine semiconductor manufacturing strength with durable quantum policy and startup execution are positioned to lead the 2030 landscape.
