# Germany

## Country status

Germany is a strong European semiconductor-spin ecosystem with policy backing and deep participation in EU-scale silicon spin-qubit industrialization programs.

## Government policy and support

- Germany's federal quantum strategy/action framework supports large-scale quantum technology development.
- German institutes are core contributors to EU silicon spin-qubit scale-up consortia (QLSI/QLSI2).

## Universities and research institutions

- Forschungszentrum Julich and Fraunhofer institutes participate in major silicon spin-qubit programs.
- German university and institute partners contribute to device, variability, and scaling research.

## Companies investing and doing R&D

- Germany's role is strongest in research institutes, process development, and industrial supply-chain participation rather than a single dominant domestic spin-qubit startup.

## Standout milestones

- QLSI2 (2024-2027) targets a cloud-accessible 200-qubit semiconductor quantum computer, with German participation across research and industrial partners.
