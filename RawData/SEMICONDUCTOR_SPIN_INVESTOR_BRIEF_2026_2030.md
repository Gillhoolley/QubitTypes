## Thesis

Semiconductor-spin is a high-conviction, medium-to-long horizon quantum hardware thesis, with unusually strong scalability logic due to compatibility with existing semiconductor manufacturing.

## Investment implications

1. Favor teams with foundry-compatible roadmaps and measured reproducibility.
2. Prioritize full-stack capabilities (control, software, deployment), not qubit metrics alone.
3. Include enabler exposure (fabrication/process and cryo-electronics ecosystems).
4. Treat high-potential/high-restriction geographies with strict risk controls.

## Scenario view

- Base: steady scaling progress, selective commercial adoption in high-value use cases.
- Upside: strong error-correction milestones and broad government procurement acceleration.
- Downside: integration complexity delays utility-scale deployment and monetization.

## Diligence checklist

- Evidence of reproducible performance across multiple devices/fabs.
- Contract conversion from pilot to recurring engagements.
- Control-stack maturity and operations footprint.
- Supply-chain resilience and export-control exposure.

## Bottom line

Semiconductor-spin offers one of the most credible routes to scalable quantum hardware by 2030, but success will depend on engineering execution and commercialization discipline as much as on physics milestones.
